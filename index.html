Automated theorem proving is an important research field crossing the studies of computer science and mathematics, and plays a major role in the verification of programs and hybrid systems. A team from Peking University, Institute of Software, CAS, ACTRI and Nanyang Technological University, aiming at attacking theoretical challenges in program verification as well as developing verification tools, has been supported by NSFC under a key project named “Theories, Algorithms and Tools of Automated Theorem Proving in Program Verification” from 2018 to 2022.

The team decided to hold a workshop every year to make it a forum for the team members and their students to exchange ideas and report their research progress. The first workshop, the kick-off of the project was held at Peking University from 24 to 25 December 2017. This year, the workshop on Program Verification and Cyber Security (PVCS 2019) will take place at Nanyang Technological University, Singapore from 2 August 2019 to 5 August, 2019. All team members from four affiliations will attend the workshop and give talks on their recent work.
